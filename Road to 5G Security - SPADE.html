OVERVIEW

5G network and Internet of Things (IoT) are foreseen as the key enabling technologies that boost the development for new applications for all vertical industries. With its high-speed transfers, low latency, and multiple simultaneous connections, 5G is not only ideal for smartphone and tablet terminal applications, but is also being looked at for smart automotive, medical/health care, national security, public infrastructure (transport and bridge, etc., monitoring), home electrical goods (cleaners, refrigerators, etc.) and more applications. Different technologies such as Software Defined Networking (SDN), Network Function Virtualization (NFV) and Multi-access Edge Computing (MEC) has identified as enabling technologies for both 5G and IoT networks.

However, as IoT devices are supposed to have high and permanent 5G network connectivity, better resources utilization and easier management, an attacker finding a vulnerability might easily target to the millions of devices at once and so to the (5G) network infrastructure. Moreover, integration of computing devices in everyday items exposes them to a potentially hostile physical environment. In this regard, it is crucial to have security in 5G wireless networks, considering the constraints imposed by heterogeneous IoT devices.
